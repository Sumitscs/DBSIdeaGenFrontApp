The current meltdown in the US is turning out to be a mixed bag with near and long-term impacts on our business.

Near term: Diminished investment income liquidity expenditure is going to be slow. There is also a predicted slow down in the Life and Annuities business, while we shall continue to see a flat to marginally upward trend in the P&C market. (Novarica Oct 2008 report on 2009 IT Budgeting and Planning)

While we would likely to be seeing a dip in the back office support functions, customer facing and web based support would be a definite area for increased activity. The early technology adopters continue to reap benefits and this trend is likely to continue.

Long Term: Insurance companies would continue to invest in IT as it is a Key differentiator in driving the costs down. IT spending would continue to be as is if not more and there is not a likelihood of any shrinkage in expenditure. (Gartner Oct 2008 report on IT spending)

Future looks to be very exciting with an increased focus from Insurance Companies towards outsourcing and, to stay in the game, many institutions would look for Acquisitions and Mergers. We are in the thick of things and poised to increase our market share with our offerings and expertise. We have a healthy pipeline, plenty of clients visiting us during the later part of Q3 and Q4. We are looking forward to closing the year on a high and pursuing some key deals.
